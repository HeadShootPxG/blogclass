How I Judge a Book By It's Cover

Please don’t judge me, but yes maybe I really do judge books by their covers.

I have to be honest: I’m not a big reader. I would like to read more, but usually I only pick up a book when something really catch my eye. And most of the time, the first thing I look at is the cover.

People always say “Don’t judge a book by its cover.” Well I dothis. Maybe it’s because I don’t read so much, so the cover help me decide very fast if the book looks interesting or maybe too difficult for me.

What Attracts Me

The Cover

I prefer covers that are simple and clean. Soft colours, drawings or something a bit artistic make me more curious. When a cover is very dark or super busy, it already give me a bad feeling and I don’t even touch the book.

The Font

Fonts are important for me. Big bold letters look too serious or even a little aggressive sometimes. I like when the title looks friendly or kind of written by hand, because then the book look more easy to read.

The Size

Thick books really scare me. When I see a huge novel, I feel tired already before I open it. Small or medium books are more ok for me, especially because I don’t read a lot in my free time.

The Author’s Name

I don’t have very strong opinion about authors, but sometimes when the name is only initials it looks more professional or serious. I don’t really judge by gender, I just see if the name sound a bit familiar or not.

Other Things I Look At

Because I’m not a strong reader, I usually choose books that I think I can really enjoy and understand without getting lost.

  • Genre: I prefer simple fiction with clear stories.
  • Format: graphic novels or books with pictures are more attractive for me.
  • Audience: young adult books feel more easy and less stressful.

Books That Surprised Me

Even if I don’t read a lot, some books still surprised me a bit, in good and sometimes in strange ways. Here are three examples.

Example 1 :

The Little Prince

At first, the cover looked very childish to me, like it was just for small kids. So I didn’t really want to read it.

But when I finally read it, the story was beautiful and also very deep. It showed me that a simple cover can hide a really strong and emotional story.

Example 2 :

Diary of a Wimpy Kid

I chose this book because of the funny cartoon style on the cover. It looks light, modern and not too serious, so I thought, “Ok, this one is for me.”

The drawings and the simple language made it really easy to follow. It helped me see that reading can be fun and not always hard work.

Example 3 :

One of Us Is Lying

The cover looked very dramatic, with big red letters and serious faces. First I thought it was too intense and probably too difficult for me.

But the story was fast and exciting, and I really wanted to know who was the killer. This book taught me that sometimes a cover that looks a bit scary or too serious can still hide a story I actually enjoy.

What I Learned

After thinking about all this, I realize some things about my own reading:

  • I depend a lot on covers because I am not a regular reader.
  • I get quickly scared by long or very serious-looking books.
  • Sometimes a book I don’t expect much from becomes one of the best ones for me.
  • A friendly and simple cover makes reading feel more possible for me.

Final Thoughts

I still judge books by their covers, but now I try to be a little bit more open. I know that sometimes the story is much better than the appearance outside.

Maybe one day I will read more regular. But for now, I have to admit: yes, the cover is still the first thing that convince me.
